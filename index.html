The Artist Behind the Lines

I am Prakash H, a self-taught hyper-realistic pencil portrait artist from Tamil Nadu, India. With years of dedicated practice, I have developed a signature style that blurs the boundary between photograph and graphite drawing.

Each stroke is intentional. Each shadow tells a story. My work explores raw human emotion — grief, joy, resilience — rendered in meticulous detail through the timeless medium of pencil on paper.

Featured Work

Fractured Emotion

This piece represents the duality of human emotion — the visible composure and the invisible fracture within. Rendered over 120 hours with 8B to 6H graphite pencils, this portrait captures a moment suspended between breaking and enduring.

Graphite on 300gsm cartridge paper 18 × 24 inches

For me, drawing is not just about creating a face on paper. It is about capturing emotion, texture, and the hidden stories within human expressions. Using graphite, I explore the subtle details of skin, light, and shadow to create portraits that feel alive. Every wrinkle, reflection, and contrast is carefully studied and rendered with patience. My goal is to transform simple pencil marks into powerful visual experiences.

Our Vision

Beyond creating artworks, I am deeply passionate about art education. Through my initiative I.D.E.A – Innovate, Design, Explore, Art, I aim to develop creative learning resources and visual arts textbooks that help students discover their artistic potential. My vision is to make art more accessible, inspiring, and innovative for the next generation of creators.
